  • Siblings may inherit if no children

Understanding Islamic Inheritance

Islamic inheritance law (Mirath) is based on the Quran and Sunnah, providing specific shares for eligible heirs. The distribution varies based on the family composition and surviving relatives. Below are the detailed schedules for different scenarios.

Special Cases and Rules

Male vs Female Inheritance

Generally, male heirs receive twice the share of female heirs in the same category (e.g., sons vs daughters). This is because males have financial responsibilities for their families.

Charitable Bequests (Wasiyya)

Up to 1/3 of the estate can be allocated to non-heirs or charities. The remaining 2/3 must be distributed according to Islamic inheritance laws.

Debts and Expenses

Before any distribution, the following must be paid in order: 1) Funeral expenses, 2) Debts, 3) Wasiyya (up to 1/3), 4) Inheritance distribution.

Exclusions

Certain individuals cannot inherit: murderers of the deceased, non-Muslims (except in specific circumstances), and slaves (historically).
